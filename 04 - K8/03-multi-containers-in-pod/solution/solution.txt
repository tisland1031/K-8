Q1. Do multiple container in a pod, share the same network stack by default?

    Yes. They share the same network namespace, meaning they share one IP address, loopback interface, and port space.

Q2. Do multiple container in a pod, share the same storage by default?

    No. Their filesystems are isolated by default. To share storage, you must explicitly configure a shared Kubernetes volume.

Q3. Does each container in pod need to have a different port number or can they be the same?

    They must have different port numbers. Since they share a single network stack, port collisions will cause failures.

Q4. Does each container in pod get a differnet ip?

    No. The Pod receives one unique IP address, which is shared by all of its internal containers simultaneously.
